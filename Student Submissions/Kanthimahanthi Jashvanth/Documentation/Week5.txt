In this module, performance trends related to ticket handling and resolution were analyzed to evaluate the efficiency of the IT support system. The primary objective was to identify patterns in resolution times, compare team performance, and detect high-priority issues that remain unresolved.

The first step involved analyzing the average resolution time based on ticket priority and ticket type. Resolution time is an important performance indicator because it reflects how quickly the support team responds to and resolves customer issues. By calculating the average resolution duration for each priority level (High, Medium, and Low), it becomes possible to determine whether critical issues are being resolved faster than lower-priority tickets. Similarly, comparing resolution times across different ticket types helps identify which types of problems take longer to resolve.

Next, the analysis focused on determining which countries or support teams handle tickets the fastest. This was done by grouping the dataset based on country or team and calculating the average resolution time for each group. Visualizations such as bar charts were used to compare performance across different regions or teams. This analysis helps highlight the most efficient support teams and identify areas where improvements may be required.

Another important aspect of this module was identifying high-priority tickets that remain unresolved. High-priority issues usually represent critical problems that may impact business operations or customer satisfaction. By filtering the dataset to detect unresolved high-priority tickets, it becomes possible to monitor service delays and ensure that urgent issues receive immediate attention.

Overall, the performance trend analysis provides valuable insights into support efficiency, team productivity, and issue management. The findings from this module help organizations improve ticket handling strategies, allocate resources more effectively, and enhance the overall customer support experience.
